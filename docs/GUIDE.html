1. Quick Start 🎯 Primary

Before the first point, walk through these six steps. The whole setup takes under two minutes if the roster is already loaded.

  1. Open the app. If a game is already active, tap the active game banner on the Home screen. Otherwise tap Game Setup.
  2. Enter the opponent name, date, start time, and field. These can be pre-filled from a scheduled game on the Home screen.
  3. Tap each player name in the check-in list to mark them present. Players not checked in are excluded from lineup suggestions.
  4. Set the gender ratio. The default is ABBA — alternates 3bx/2gx ↔ 2bx/3gx every point. Tap "More presets" or "Custom pattern" for fixed ratios.
  5. Record the pre-game flip: who won, whether Marmots will pull or receive first, and which end zone they attack to start.
  6. Tap Start Game at the bottom. The live game view opens immediately.

What to look for

  1. 1 Opponent name and date fields at the top of the form.
  2. 2 Check-in list — tap a player row to toggle them present or absent; present players show a checkmark.
  3. 3 Ratio picker — ABBA is selected by default; tap "More presets" or "Custom pattern" for alternatives.
  4. 4 Gender flip section — record who won the flip and which end zone Marmots attack first.
  5. 5 The large Start Game button at the very bottom of the screen.

2. Running a Point 🎯 Primary

Between points, the app is in pre-point phase. This is where you review and finalize the lineup before releasing the disc.

Pre-point workflow

  • The app auto-suggests 5 players — equalizing points played (or minutes, if time mode is on), respecting the current gender ratio, and mixing grades where possible.
  • To manually swap a player: tap the ↓ arrow on an on-field player to move them to the bench, then tap the ↑ arrow on the bench player to bring them on — or vice versa.
  • Tap SIT on an on-field player to exclude them from auto-pick this point without fully substituting them. They move to a "Sitting out" section and won't be suggested by Auto-pick.
  • Tap the ratio badge (e.g. "3bx / 2gx") to cycle the ratio for this point only (3/2 → 2/3 → 4/1 → 1/4 → …).
  • Tap Auto-pick to re-run the suggestion algorithm with your current bench and any SIT exclusions applied.
  • The direction bar shows the current attack direction and whether Marmots pull or receive (e.g. "→ Marmots attack right · we pull").
  • When the lineup is set, tap Start Point. The game clock starts and the phase shifts to playing.
📱 Screenshot: Pre-point view — lineup, bench, direction bar, action bar to be added

What to look for

  1. 1 Direction indicator at the top — arrow showing attack direction and pull/receive status.
  2. 2 On Field section — 5 players each showing their playing time stats; SIT button removes them from auto-pick.
  3. 3 Ratio badge (e.g. "3bx / 2gx") — tap to cycle the ratio for this point.
  4. 4 Bench section — sorted by most rest needed first; teal left border marks the least-played player.
  5. 5 Auto-pick and Start Point buttons in the action bar at the bottom.

3. Scoring & Timeouts 🎯 Primary

Once a point is started, the app enters playing phase. The point clock is running.

Ending a point

  • Tap We Scored (green) when Marmots catch the disc in the end zone.
  • Tap They Scored (red) when the opponent scores.
  • After scoring, an 8-second Undo banner appears — tap it to revert the last point if you tapped the wrong button.

Timeouts

  • Tap Timeout to pause the game clock and enter timeout-sub phase.
  • During the timeout you can swap players exactly as you would in pre-point phase — adjust, add stats, review.
  • The scoreboard shows a pulsing TIMEOUT label and a banner reads "TIMEOUT — adjust lineup for resume."
  • Tap Resume Point when play restarts. The clock begins fresh for the new segment.

Correcting a lineup mid-point

  • If you realize the wrong player is listed on field, use the FIX button — a two-tap swap that corrects data entry without affecting the point clock.
  • Tap FIX on the player who should be coming off, then tap IN on the correct replacement in the bench list.
📱 Screenshot: Playing phase — scoreboard, action bar with We Scored / They Scored / Timeout to be added

What to look for

  1. 1 Scoreboard — Marmots score on the left, opponent on the right, game clock, and point clock.
  2. 2 We Scored (green) and They Scored (red) — large tap targets in the action bar.
  3. 3 Timeout button — pauses the clock and opens lineup adjustment.
  4. 4 FIX button on player rows — tap to start a two-tap correction of the lineup mid-point.
  5. 5 Undo banner — appears for 8 seconds after a score; tap to revert if you pressed the wrong button.

4. Halftime & Game End 🎯 Primary

Halftime

  • The app alerts when either team reaches 6 points or the game clock hits 30 minutes — whichever comes first.
  • Tap Take Half on the alert banner, or tap the Halftime button in the action bar during pre-point phase.
  • A halftime overlay appears showing the current score. Tap Resume Play when the second half is ready to start.
  • The field direction indicator flips automatically for the second half — no manual adjustment needed.

Ending the game

  • The game clock turns orange at 25 min and red at 55 min. A red danger banner appears at 60 min (hard cap).
  • Tap End Game in the action bar any time during pre-point phase.
  • A Game Summary modal opens — review the final score and top stats before committing.
  • Tap Confirm & Save. The game is written to history and a sync to Google Sheets fires automatically.
The app auto-saves every state change — if the phone dies or the browser closes mid-game, reopen the app and it restores from where you left off.

5. Point History Strip 🎯 Primary

The point strip sits just below the scoreboard and gives you a scrollable timeline of every point in the game.

  • Green chip = Marmots scored that point.
  • Red chip = Opponent scored that point.
  • Gray chips (future) = Projected upcoming lineups — not yet played.
  • Tap any past chip to review that point's lineup, stats earned, direction, and timing.
  • In the past-point detail view, you can also edit who scored (Us/Them) if an entry was wrong.
  • Tap ← Back to Current Point in the action bar to return to the live game view.

What to look for

  1. 1 Point strip — scroll left/right; green = Marmots scored, red = opponent scored, gray = future.
  2. 2 Past point detail — shows the full lineup on field and all stats earned that point.
  3. 3 Direction info in the detail view — which way Marmots attacked and whether they pulled or received.
  4. 4 ← Back to Current Point button in the action bar to return to the live game.

6. Adding Player Stats 📊 Secondary

Stats are optional — you can track none, some, or all. They work during a live point and on any past point.

  • Tap anywhere on a player row to open the stat modal for that player.
  • Available stats: Goal, Assist, D (block or defensive play), Great Throw.
  • Tap a stat button to add it — tap it again to remove it. There's no save step.
  • Stat chips appear on the player row: D = blue, A = yellow, G = green.
  • This works for the current point (during playing phase) and for any past point — tap that point's chip in the strip first, then tap the player row.
  • Tap the (i) button at the end of a bench player's row to open their player info card — grade, gender, and game totals.

What to look for

  1. 1 Tap the whole player bar — anywhere on the row opens the stat picker for that player.
  2. 2 Goal, Assist, D, Great Throw buttons — tap to add, tap again to remove. No save needed.
  3. 3 (i) button at the end of each bench row — opens the player info card with grade, gender, and cumulative stats.
  4. 4 Stat chips on player rows — colored at a glance: D = blue, A = yellow, G = green.

7. Spectator / Parent View 📊 Secondary

Parents and spectators can follow the game live from their own phone — no login or account needed.

  • During a game, tap Share Live Link in the top-right of the game view. The link is copied to your clipboard.
  • Send the link via text message or group chat.
  • Parents open the link in any browser — they see the live score, the current on-field lineup, and recent points.
  • The spectator view updates in real time. No manual refresh required.
  • The link is read-only — spectators cannot make any changes to the game.

What to look for

  4. 4 Auto-refreshes in real time — no login or reload required.

8. Game Setup ⚙️ Setup

Out-of-game preparation before game day:

  • Roster is managed in the Roster screen — add, edit, or delete players individually, or use Import from Google Sheets to bulk-load from a shared spreadsheet (Name, Gender, Grade columns required).
  • Scheduled games can be added from the Home screen. Tap a scheduled game to pre-fill the setup form with opponent, date, time, and field.
  • Check in players, set ratio, and record the flip as part of the normal game-start flow described in Section 1.
  • The app auto-saves every state change to Firestore. If the browser closes or the phone is locked mid-game, reopening the app fully restores the game.

9. Past Games ⚙️ Setup

All completed games are stored and viewable in the Past Games screen:

  • Tap a game row to expand it — see the final score, all points played, and the lineup for each point.
  • Tap Edit on an individual point to correct who scored (Us or Them) or to delete a point that was entered incorrectly.
  • Tap Export CSV on any game to download a summary file for reporting or archiving.
  • Games can be soft-deleted — they disappear from the list but are not permanently removed.

10. Game End Rules ⚙️ Setup

Quick reference — also accessible from the Home screen via Game End Rules:

  • Win condition: First team to 11 points wins.
  • Halftime: First team to reach 6 points, OR the 30-minute wall-clock cap — whichever comes first.
  • Hard cap: At 60 minutes, finish the current point. If still tied, play a universe point.
  • 70-minute rule: Each team gets one more possession after the 70-minute mark. A tie is possible.

The app automatically alerts you at each threshold and adjusts the scoreboard clock colors: orange at 25 min, red at 55 min, danger banner at 60 min.
